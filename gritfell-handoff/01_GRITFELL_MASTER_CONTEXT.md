# 01 — GRITFELL MASTER CONTEXT

## 1. Brand definition

GritFell is an outdoor-lifestyle apparel brand centered on fishing, hunting, water, woods, wildlife, camp, trail, and weekend outdoor life.

**Positioning sentence:**  
GritFell makes pursuit-rooted apparel for outdoorsmen who carry the water, woods, and first-light ritual into everyday life.

### GritFell is

- Outdoor lifestyle, not technical equipment.
- Pursuit identity, not generic nature decoration.
- Specific species, places, rituals, and insider moments.
- Rugged, calm, masculine, giftable, and field-to-town.
- Premium-looking but accessible.
- A modular world across button shirts, polos, swim shorts, hats, and future layers.

### GritFell is not

- Tactical/operator apparel.
- Loud meme merchandise.
- Generic patriotic souvenir merchandise.
- Western/Texana by default.
- Cute cottagecore.
- A performance authority without product proof.
- A permanent-discount POD store.
- A clone of Drake, Duck Camp, BURLEBO, Huk, or SITKA.

## 2. Audience architecture

### Primary wearer: Tom

- Male, approximately 45–65.
- U.S. suburban, exurban, small-town, Southern, Midwestern, Mountain West, or coastal outdoor markets.
- Fishes, hunts, camps, hikes, boats, trains a hunting dog, or participates in several pursuits.
- Wears outdoor identity apparel to work, errands, church, cookouts, travel, camp, boat, and casual weekends.
- Values clear species and pursuit signals.
- Wants recognition without shouting.

### Primary gift buyer: Megan

- Wife, partner, daughter, or family member shopping for Tom.
- May not know technical equipment, but understands his dog, favorite species, season, destination, and stories.
- Needs a gift specific enough to communicate: “I know what you care about.”
- Responds to emotional accuracy, clear product imagery, easy sizing, reviews, shipping clarity, and bundles.

### High-value sub-personas

1. Waterfowl + retriever man.
2. Freshwater angler.
3. Inshore angler.
4. Big-game hunter.
5. Outdoor dad passing traditions down.
6. Professional/tradesman who lives outside on weekends.
7. Hiker/camp man focused on first light, summit, trail, fire, and coffee.

## 4. Brand voice

**Earned, not loud. Specific, not generic. Warm, not sentimental. Masculine, not forced.**

### Preferred vocabulary

built, stitched, worn, earned, field, water, woods, marsh, blind, stand, camp, truck, first light, opener, season, retrieve, river, ridge, tide, passed down, ready, return, tradition, partner, home water

### Avoid

best ever, ultimate, epic, badass, operator, warrior, domination, generic “adventure awaits,” generic “nature is healing,” excessive exclamation points, crowded puns, unsupported performance superlatives

### Copy structure

1. Short declarative headline.
2. Precise cultural detail.
3. Emotional truth.
4. Product reason.
5. Clear CTA.

Example:

> **BUILT FOR THE FIRST LIGHT.**  
> For the man who is packed before the alarm goes off. Lightweight comfort, field-to-town wearability, and a pattern his hunting buddies recognize first.

## 5. Visual DNA

### Core palette

- Bone White `#F4F1EA`
- Iron Charcoal `#2A2A28`
- Aged Tan `#C9A87A`
- Rust Orange `#B8521E`
- Forest Green `#2D4D3A`

Supporting colors: washed navy, sand, olive, black, muted blue, marsh brown, off-white.

### Principles

- Earthy and desaturated.
- One hero subject with limited support details.
- Specific species anatomy or gear cues.
- Strong negative space.
- Readable from a distance.
- Mockup environment must match the story.
- No generic clipart combinations.
- No random patriotic color injection.
- Hats favor identity badges; shirts can carry richer worldbuilding.

### Background map

| Niche | Environment | Props |
|---|---|---|
| Waterfowl | blind, flooded timber, marsh, tailgate | decoy, thermos, dog lead |
| Freshwater | creek, dock, lake cabin | lure, creel, fly line, reel |

## 6. Locked Idea Engine architecture

### Layer 1 — Idea Engine

`niche → sub_niche → subject_treatment → composition → background_scene + axes`

### Layer 2 — Medium Translator

- Hat: compact embroidery, limited colors, production constraints.
- Shirt: larger canvas, richer scene, print/AOP options.
- It translates an idea; it does not create a new idea.

### Layer 3 — Brand Filter

Applies GritFell voice, palette, allowed niches, and forbidden rules.

### Core niches

- Hunting: mallard, pintail, wood duck, goose, whitetail, elk, turkey, upland, bird dog.
- Fishing: largemouth, smallmouth, trout species, walleye, crappie, redfish, fly fishing, tackle.
- Camp/trail: cabin, canoe, lantern, compass, fire, lake camp.
- Mountain: named ridge, alpine lake, fire lookout, trail, topo.
- Birding: naturalist species studies without slogan-heavy novelty.
- Quiet region/place: named water, range, county, or tonal map.

### Hard sub-niche rule

Use specific subjects such as “drake mallard banking into flooded timber,” not “duck”; “rainbow trout,” not “fish.”

### Subject treatments

1. Single animal hero.
2. Habitat vignette.
3. Object/gear hero.
4. Region/place.
5. Action/narrative.
6. Double exposure or negative space, mainly for shirts.

### Strongest locked creative lanes

- Waterfowl in flight.
- Naturalist species portrait.
- Tonal region/place.
- Custom mountain/place identity.
- Pursuit ritual and calendar moments.
- Dog + hunter partnership.

### Underdeveloped lanes

- Object/gear hero: decoy, fly box, creel, shell row, tackle.
- Big-game/upland action: elk bugle, turkey strut, dog on point, retrieve.
- Cross-product collection systems.

## 7. Embroidery production rules

- Front zone: approximately 7 × 13 cm.
- Target file: 1455 × 855 px at 300 DPI.
- Ideal stitch count: about 3–4K.
- Minimum line weight: 4–5 mm.
- Default thread colors: 3; fourth only for a meaningful “money detail.”
- No gradient, ombre, catchlight, blur, or painterly blending.
- Motion is shown with stitched lines, not blur.
- Tone-on-tone `tonal: true` is preferred for quiet premium designs.

### Stitch styles

| Code | Style | Best use |
|---|---|---|
| K1 | Bold neo-traditional | bass, hog, mascot, strong silhouettes |
| K2 | Cross-hatch naturalist | default for deer, elk, trout, dog, waterfowl |
| K3 | Sparse line art | minimal, high negative space |
| K4 | Mascot badge | clean vector, hard satin edges |
| K5 | Hyper-realism | sweatshirt only, not hats |

## 8. Brand filter

### Eligible

- Hunting, especially waterfowl in flight.
- Fishing with species accuracy.
- Camp, mountain, trail.
- Naturalist birding without novelty slogans.
- Quiet named place and tonal region.
- Field-to-town identity.
- Restrained calendar/seasonal ritual when authentic.

### Reject or route elsewhere

- Pun-heavy and affirmation-heavy slogans.
- Patriotic/RWB/1776 as core.
- Cause/disaster merchandise.
- Western/Texana icons by default.
- Cute, kawaii, insect whimsy.
- Camo imitation as the main differentiation.
- Saturated state + flower + EST templates.
- Modern hype/streetwear voice.
- Fake technical claims.
