## 3. Creative format templates

### Static ad

1. Hero person in real environment.
2. Product close-up.
3. One diagnostic pattern inset.
4. Headline under seven words.
5. Offer as secondary information.
6. One CTA.

### 15-second video

- 0–2s: cultural hook.
- 2–5s: real pursuit environment.
- 5–9s: product worn and detail close-up.
- 9–12s: material/fit proof.
- 12–15s: collection or offer + CTA.

Example:
- 0–2s: Truck clock reads 4:31 AM.
- 2–5s: Dog jumps into the back.
- 5–9s: Mallard-pattern shirt close-up.
- 9–12s: Lightweight movement shot.
- 12–15s: “BUILT BEFORE DAYLIGHT — SHOP NOW.”

### UGC gift ad

- Wife POV.
- Show his truck/dog/gear without mocking him.
- Reveal product.
- Capture reaction or expected reaction.
- Use specific rather than generic language.

Hook:
> “I stopped searching ‘gift for outdoorsman’ and searched for the thing he actually talks about.”

The agent should test angles, not merely swap synonyms.

---

## 5. Copy guardrails

- Do not promise moisture wicking, UPF, antimicrobial, cooling technology, or technical field protection without verified product evidence.
- Do not call imitation linen “linen.”
- Do not use fake scarcity.
- Do not overuse “heritage” without product and visual support.
- Do not use firearm handling as a casual visual prop.
- Avoid competitor names in customer-facing copy.
- Do not turn wives into caricatures or hunters into stereotypes.
- Keep humor observational, not demeaning.
